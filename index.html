Why Custer?

Custer, discover the wild beauty

Custer, South Dakota is an ideal destination for adventurers seeking a thrilling and awe-inspiring experience. Nestled in the heart of the Black Hills, Custer offers an array of outdoor activities that cater to adventure enthusiasts. One of the main attractions is Custer State Park, a sprawling wilderness with rugged landscapes, towering granite peaks, and winding trails perfect for hiking, rock climbing, and camping. Adventurers can also explore the majestic granite spires of Needles Highway, renowned for its challenging rock climbing routes. Wildlife enthusiasts can witness herds of bison, elk, and bighorn sheep that roam freely in the park, making for an exhilarating wildlife viewing experience. The nearby Crazy Horse Memorial, the world's largest mountain carving in progress, offers thrilling opportunities for hiking and learning about Native American culture. Additionally, the iconic Mount Rushmore National Memorial is just a short drive away, providing an unforgettable adventure with its grandiose sculptures and historic significance. Whether it's hiking, rock climbing, camping, wildlife viewing, or cultural exploration, Custer, South Dakota offers a thrilling and unforgettable adventure for outdoor enthusiasts.

Favorite attractions around Custer

Mount Rushmore

Mount Rushmore is a colossal sculpture carved into the granite face of the Black Hills. It features the carved faces of four U.S. presidents - George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln - and is a world-renowned symbol of American history and patriotism.

Crazy Horse Memorial

The Crazy Horse Memorial is a massive ongoing mountain carving. It depicts the legendary Lakota Sioux leader, Crazy Horse, on horseback and is a tribute to Native American culture, art, and history, making it one of the most significant cultural landmarks in the United States.
